## Design Context

### Users

Drive-In is used by Tesla occupants on a large in-car touchscreen. The primary job is to start and control Canvas-rendered media with as little typing and precision tapping as possible. Controls must also remain usable from a phone-sized browser.

### Brand Personality

Calm, direct, cinematic. The interface should feel dependable and purpose-built for a car, not like a developer dashboard or a generic streaming clone.

### Aesthetic Direction

A restrained, cool-tinted dark interface with high contrast, large touch targets, and sparse typography. Functional state is more important than decoration. Avoid neon effects, busy cards, tiny controls, and modal-heavy settings.

### Design Principles

- Prefer one-tap choices over typed configuration.
- Make current state visible before the user acts.
- Use at least 48px touch targets for primary in-car controls.
- Keep playback controls visually quiet until needed.
- Preserve a stable layout across Tesla and phone viewports.
